Pillar 1 — Compliance-ready

Your accessibility and AI-governance leaders can sign on first read.

  • Custom Canvas — program managers and policy staff build the dashboards they need inside your product. No data exports, no shadow tools. Every chart respects the access rules your data team already runs.
  • Dashboards as Code — every dashboard lives as a reviewable file in your codebase. Every change has an author, a reviewer, and a history your auditors can read.
  • AI chat — staff get answers and charts on demand without leaving your product and without copy-pasting citizen data into a personal AI subscription. Row-level access rules apply to every question, so no one sees a record they aren't authorised to see. Every conversation is logged.

Pillar 2 — AI-built, never AI-exposed

The AI helps your people build. The AI never sees the citizen.

The model sees the question and the shape of the data. The model never sees the rows.

  • Custom Canvas — staff describe the dashboard they want; AI assembles it from datasets and chart templates you have already approved. Generated charts land on the canvas the user is already looking at.
  • Dashboards as Code — AI proposes the dashboard definition; a human reviews the file before it ever runs. AI proposes, your team disposes.
  • AI chat — users ask in plain language and get a text answer plus a chart, which they save to their canvas with one click. The model sees the question and the schema; the model never sees the records. You pick which AI — a government-cloud model, an on-premise model, whichever your agency has already added to its inventory.
