## The problem

Your operations leaders want AI in predictive maintenance, quality, and throughput. Your OT security team won't approve a tool that reaches out from the plant network to an external model — the boundary between operational technology and the outside is the whole point. Your IP and legal teams won't approve anything that risks leaking process and recipe data that is the company's core trade secret. So the project stalls between the AI sponsor, the OT boundary, and the IP owner, and the rollout slips a quarter. Most analytics vendors assume a chatty cloud; almost none respect an OT boundary and arrive with paperwork your security lead will read. The clock on board-level AI commitments and tightening industrial-security expectations is already running.

## Pillar 1 — Compliance-ready

### Your OT security and IP leaders can say yes on first read.

- **Custom Canvas** — engineers and plant staff build the dashboards they need *inside* your environment, on your side of the OT boundary. No data exports, no shadow tools. Every chart obeys the access rules your team already enforces.
- **Dashboards as Code** — every dashboard lives as a reviewable, version-controlled file in your codebase. Every change has an author, a reviewer, and a history — the documented control trail your IEC 62443 and NIS2 posture already expect.
- **AI chat** — staff get answers and charts on demand without leaving your environment and without pasting process data into a personal AI subscription. Row-level access rules apply to every question, so no one sees data they aren't authorised to see. Every conversation is logged.

## Pillar 2 — AI-built, never AI-exposed

### The AI helps your people build. The AI never crosses the OT boundary.

The model sees the *question* and the *shape* of the data. The model never sees the rows — and never reaches outside the plant.

- **Custom Canvas** — staff describe the dashboard they want; AI assembles it from datasets and chart templates you have already approved. Generated charts land on the canvas the user is already looking at — process data and OT telemetry stay inside the boundary.
- **Dashboards as Code** — AI proposes the dashboard definition; a human reviews the file before it ever runs. AI proposes, your team disposes.
- **AI chat** — users ask in plain language and get a text answer plus a chart, saved to their canvas with one click. The model sees the question and the schema; the model never sees the telemetry, the recipes, or the process secrets. You pick which AI — an on-premise model inside the plant, a model in your own cloud tenant, whichever your OT security team has already cleared.

**Visual:** Two columns labelled clearly. "What AI sees: the question, the schema, your design system." "What AI never sees: process and recipe data, OT telemetry, trade secrets, credentials."
