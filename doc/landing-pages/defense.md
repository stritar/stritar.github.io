## The problem

Your defense and intelligence programs want AI in mission and operations analytics. Your security officer won't approve a tool that reaches out to an external model from inside a controlled network — and on a classified or air-gapped system, it simply can't. Your export-control officer won't approve anything that risks moving controlled technical data across a boundary. So the capability stalls between the program's AI ambition, the security boundary, and the export-control line, and the milestone slips. Most analytics vendors assume a chatty cloud and external model calls; almost none can run cleanly inside your boundary with the paperwork your authorising official will read. The clock on AI commitments is already running.

## Pillar 1 — Compliance-ready

### Your security and export-control leaders can say yes on first read.

- **Custom Canvas** — analysts and program staff build the dashboards they need *inside* your environment, on your side of the boundary. No data exports, no shadow tools. Every chart obeys the access rules your security team already enforces.
- **Dashboards as Code** — every dashboard lives as a reviewable, version-controlled file in your codebase. Every change has an author, a reviewer, and a history — the documented control trail your CMMC and NIST 800-171 assessments already expect.
- **AI chat** — staff get answers and charts on demand without leaving your environment and without reaching a personal AI subscription. Row-level access rules apply to every question, so no one sees data they aren't cleared to see. Every conversation is logged.

## Pillar 2 — AI-built, never AI-exposed

### The AI helps your people build. The AI never crosses the boundary.

The model sees the *question* and the *shape* of the data. The model never sees the rows — and never sits outside your network.

- **Custom Canvas** — staff describe the dashboard they want; AI assembles it from datasets and chart templates you have already approved. Generated charts land on the canvas the user is already looking at — controlled data never leaves the boundary.
- **Dashboards as Code** — AI proposes the dashboard definition; a human reviews the file before it ever runs. AI proposes, your team disposes.
- **AI chat** — users ask in plain language and get a text answer plus a chart, saved to their canvas with one click. The model sees the question and the schema; the model never sees the records or the controlled technical data. You pick which AI — an on-premise model inside your air-gapped enclave, a model in your sovereign cloud, whichever your authorising official has already cleared.
