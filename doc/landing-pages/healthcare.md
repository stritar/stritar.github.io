## Pillar 1 — Compliance-ready

### Your safety and privacy leaders can say yes on first read.

- **Custom Canvas** — clinical leads and operations staff build the dashboards they need *inside* your product. No data exports, no shadow tools. Every chart obeys the access rules your data team already runs.
- **Dashboards as Code** — every dashboard lives as a reviewable file in your codebase. Every change has an author, a reviewer, and a history your auditors can read.
- **AI chat** — staff get answers and charts on demand without leaving your product and without copy-pasting patient questions into a personal AI subscription. Row-level access rules apply to every question, so no one sees a record they aren't authorised to see. Every conversation is logged.

**Visual:** One dated document pack — accessibility, privacy, security, supply-chain. Most vendors deliver two of four; ours delivers four of four.

## Pillar 2 — AI-built, never AI-exposed

### The AI helps your people build. The AI never sees the patient.

The model sees the *question* and the *shape* of the data. The model never sees the rows.

- **Custom Canvas** — staff describe the dashboard they want; AI assembles it from datasets and chart templates you have already approved. Generated charts land on the canvas the user is already looking at — patient data stays inside the hospital.
- **Dashboards as Code** — AI proposes the dashboard definition; a human reviews the file before it ever runs. AI proposes, your team disposes.
- **AI chat** — users ask in plain language and get a text answer plus a chart. They save the chart to their canvas with one click. The model sees the question and the schema; the model never sees the records. You pick which AI — Azure OpenAI in your tenant, an on-premise model, whichever your CISO has already cleared.

**Visual:** Two columns labelled clearly. "What AI sees: the question, the schema, your design system." "What AI never sees: patient records, identifiers, clinical notes, credentials."
