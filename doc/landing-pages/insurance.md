## The problem

Your executives want AI in underwriting, claims, and customer service. Your model-risk and compliance teams won't approve a tool that exposes policyholder or claims data to an external model — and they're now reading every AI feature through a high-risk lens. Your data protection officer won't approve anything that moves customer data outside cleared systems. So the deal stalls between the AI sponsor, the model-risk committee, and the privacy office, and the renewal slips a quarter. Most analytics vendors answer one of those desks; none answer all of them on the same day with the same paperwork. The clock on board-level AI commitments — and on tightening governance expectations — is already running.

## Pillar 1 — Compliance-ready

### Your model-risk and privacy leaders can say yes on first read.

- **Custom Canvas** — actuaries, claims analysts, and operations staff build the dashboards they need *inside* your product. No data exports, no shadow spreadsheets. Every chart obeys the access rules your data team already runs.
- **Dashboards as Code** — every dashboard lives as a reviewable, version-controlled file in your codebase. Every change has an author, a reviewer, and a history — the documented control trail your model-risk and Solvency II governance already expect.
- **AI chat** — staff get answers and charts on demand without leaving your product and without pasting policyholder questions into a personal AI subscription. Row-level access rules apply to every question, so no one sees a record they aren't authorised to see. Every conversation is logged.

## Pillar 2 — AI-built, never AI-exposed

### The AI helps your people build. The AI never sees the policyholder.

The model sees the *question* and the *shape* of the data. The model never sees the rows.

- **Custom Canvas** — staff describe the dashboard they want; AI assembles it from datasets and chart templates you have already approved. Generated charts land on the canvas the user is already looking at — policyholder and claims data stays inside your systems.
- **Dashboards as Code** — AI proposes the dashboard definition; a human reviews the file before it ever runs. AI proposes, your team disposes — and the AI that builds a dashboard is cleanly separate from the models your committee already governs for pricing and underwriting.
- **AI chat** — users ask in plain language and get a text answer plus a chart, saved to their canvas with one click. The model sees the question and the schema; the model never sees the records, the claims history, or the underwriting data. You pick which AI — a model in your own cloud tenant, an on-premise model, whichever your risk and security teams have already cleared.

**Visual:** Two columns labelled clearly. "What AI sees: the question, the schema, your design system." "What AI never sees: policyholder records, claims, underwriting and health data, actuarial tables."
