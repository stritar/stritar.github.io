## The problem

Your leadership wants AI in the learning platform and the administrative back office. Your privacy office won't approve a tool that exposes student records — especially for minors — to an external model. Your accessibility team won't approve charts that students and staff using assistive technology can't read, and the legal exposure on that is only growing. So the project stalls between the AI sponsor, the privacy office, and the accessibility team, and the rollout slips a term. Most analytics vendors answer one of those desks; none answer all of them on the same day with the same paperwork. The clock on accessibility obligations and board-level AI commitments is already running.

## Pillar 1 — Compliance-ready

### Your privacy and accessibility leaders can say yes on first read.

- **Custom Canvas** — faculty and administrators build the dashboards they need *inside* your product. No data exports, no shadow tools. Every chart obeys the access rules your data team already runs, so a teacher only ever sees their own students.
- **Dashboards as Code** — every dashboard lives as a reviewable, version-controlled file in your codebase. Every change has an author, a reviewer, and a history your auditors can read — the kind of record FERPA reviews expect.
- **AI chat** — staff get answers and charts on demand without leaving your product and without pasting student questions into a personal AI subscription. Row-level access rules apply to every question, so no one sees a record they aren't authorised to see. Every conversation is logged. Charts are built to a WCAG 2.2 AA target so students using assistive technology aren't left out.

**Visual:** One dated document pack — accessibility, privacy, security, supply-chain. Most vendors deliver two of four; ours delivers four of four.

## Pillar 2 — AI-built, never AI-exposed

### The AI helps your people build. The AI never sees the student.

The model sees the *question* and the *shape* of the data. The model never sees the rows.

- **Custom Canvas** — staff describe the dashboard they want; AI assembles it from datasets and chart templates you have already approved. Generated charts land on the canvas the user is already looking at — student records stay inside your systems.
- **Dashboards as Code** — AI proposes the dashboard definition; a human reviews the file before it ever runs. AI proposes, your team disposes.
- **AI chat** — users ask in plain language and get a text answer plus a chart, saved to their canvas with one click. The model sees the question and the schema; the model never sees the records, the grades, or any minor's personal data. You pick which AI — a model in your own cloud tenant, an on-premise model, whichever your privacy and security teams have already cleared.
