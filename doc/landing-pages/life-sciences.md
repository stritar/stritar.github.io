Your leadership wants AI across clinical operations, manufacturing, and commercial. Your quality and validation teams won't approve a tool that puts patient-level trial data in front of an external model. Your privacy office won't approve anything that moves subject data outside the systems they've already cleared. Most analytics vendors answer one of these and shrug at the rest — so the project stalls between quality, privacy, and validation, and the AI roadmap slips a release. ## Pillar 1 — Compliance-ready

### Your quality and validation leaders can say yes on first read.

- **Custom Canvas** — data managers and operations staff build the dashboards they need *inside* your validated environment. No exports into spreadsheets, no shadow tools. Every chart obeys the access rules your data team already enforces.
- **Dashboards as Code** — every dashboard lives as a reviewable, version-controlled file in your codebase. Every change has an author, a reviewer, and a history — the kind of traceability your 21 CFR Part 11 and Annex 11 validation already expects.
- **AI chat** — staff get answers and charts on demand without leaving your product and without pasting trial questions into a personal AI subscription. Row-level access rules apply to every question, so no one sees a record they aren't authorised to see. Every conversation is logged.

## Pillar 2 — AI-built, never AI-exposed

### The AI helps your people build. The AI never sees the subject.

The model sees the *question* and the *shape* of the data. The model never sees the rows.

- **Custom Canvas** — staff describe the dashboard they want; AI assembles it from datasets and chart templates you have already approved. Generated charts land on the canvas the user is already looking at — trial and patient data stays inside the validated environment.
- **Dashboards as Code** — AI proposes the dashboard definition; a human reviews the file before it ever runs and before it enters your validated baseline. AI proposes, your team disposes.
- **AI chat** — users ask in plain language and get a text answer plus a chart, saved to their canvas with one click. The model sees the question and the schema; the model never sees the records, the subject identifiers, or the molecule. You pick which AI — a model in your own cloud tenant, an on-premise model, whichever your quality and security teams have already cleared.

**Visual:** Two columns labelled clearly. "What AI sees: the question, the schema, your design system." "What AI never sees: trial-subject records, patient-level data, identifiers, molecule and IP data."
