# 18 — The Agent Shift, in Plain English

**What this is:** A short, non-technical explainer of Jozef's hypothesis about AI agents — and how Embeddable fits it. No jargon. Created 31 May 2026. For the detailed version, see [17 — Agent-Native Rendering](./17-agent-native-rendering.md).

---

**Jozef's hunch, in one breath.** More and more, people get information by asking an AI assistant — ChatGPT, Claude, and the like — instead of opening a website. If that keeps going, the AI assistant becomes the new "browser," and the place people look at their charts and dashboards shifts from a web page into the AI's own window. The catch: an answer the AI draws by itself looks generic — it doesn't carry a company's colours, logo, or style. So companies will start wanting their brand to show up *inside* the AI experience too. Jozef's question is whether Embeddable could be the thing that makes that happen — the analytics layer that looks on-brand even when it appears inside an AI assistant.

**The short answer: yes, and we've quietly been heading there already.** This isn't a left-turn for us. It's the natural next step of the AI work we've already mapped out. We've been saying that AI is great for *helping build* a dashboard, while the company keeps full control of its data. Jozef is pointing at the same idea, one step further along: not just building the dashboard with AI, but *showing* it inside the AI. Same strategy, new window.

**Where we fit, part one — the brand travels with the charts.** Our charts and components are built so they automatically take on a company's own look: their colours, fonts, spacing, logo feel. We don't hand-paint each one; the styling is baked in and swaps to match whoever is using it. That means our charts look like "theirs" wherever they show up — on a web page today, and inside an AI assistant tomorrow. That is exactly the "make it branded in the agent" outcome Jozef is describing, and it's already how we work.

**Where we fit, part two — the part nobody else has.** Here's the difference that matters most. With our approach, the AI can put the dashboard together, but the actual data — the real numbers, customer records, sensitive information — is fetched by the company's own systems and never handed to the AI. The AI draws the picture; it doesn't get to see what's behind it. For the customers we care about most — banks, hospitals, government, defence — this is the whole ballgame. They've been told they can't let outside AI tools touch their data. We're the rare option that lets them have the AI experience *and* keep the data locked down. The other agent tools out there don't have a clear answer to that; we do.

**The honest caveats.** Two things to be straight about. First, we can't render inside an AI assistant *today* — our charts currently live on web pages, so making them appear inside an agent is new work we'd have to build (not a rebuild, but a genuine new piece). Second, the standards for doing this (the ones Jozef linked — Google's A2UI and the MCP apps Claude supports) are brand new and still changing, so it's smart to experiment, not to bet the farm yet. We'd also want to finish the accessibility and audit-trail work we've already planned first, so anything we show in an agent is just as usable and trustworthy as what we ship today.

**Bottom line.** Jozef's instinct is right, and it lines up neatly with where we were already going. Our brand-matching charts are the "looks native in the agent" piece, and our keep-the-data-safe approach is the trust piece that makes it sellable to serious customers. The one thing we don't yet know is whether a real customer actually wants this *right now* — and that's the signal worth watching. If a prospect asks for "our analytics, inside the AI assistant," that's the green light to build it.
