## Overview

Aurora is a digital-first bank serving 2.4M customers across Europe. Their
flagship app had grown by accretion — every team shipped its own patterns — and
the result was a product that tested well in isolation but fell apart as a
whole. I led a six-month redesign covering research, a new design system, and
the end-to-end flows for payments, savings, and onboarding.

> The redesign moved our app store rating from 3.6 to 4.7 and cut onboarding
> drop-off by more than a third. — VP of Product, Aurora

## The challenge

Three problems compounded each other:

- **Fragmentation.** Eleven button styles, four date pickers, and no shared
  spacing scale meant every screen felt subtly off.
- **Cognitive load.** The home screen surfaced 23 actions with equal weight.
- **Trust.** Users abandoned high-value flows — moving money, opening accounts —
  at the exact moments the bank earned revenue.

<Reveal>
  We reframed the brief from "make it prettier" to "make every screen earn the
  next tap." Each flow had to justify its existence against a single metric:
  completed, trusted actions.
</Reveal>

## Approach

I ran the work in three overlapping tracks.

### 1. A token-driven design system

We rebuilt the foundations as a set of semantic tokens — color, type, spacing,
motion — then expressed every component against them. The system shipped as code
and Figma libraries in lockstep, so design and engineering never drifted.

### 2. Progressive disclosure

The new home screen leads with one primary action and a glanceable balance, then
reveals depth on demand. Secondary actions live one layer down, surfaced
contextually rather than crammed onto a grid.

### 3. Choreographed trust

High-stakes flows got deliberate motion and microcopy: confirmations that feel
considered, transitions that show where money goes, and states that never leave
the user guessing.

## Outcomes

After a phased rollout to 100% of users over eight weeks:

- **+38%** task completion on core money-movement flows
- **−52%** support tickets related to navigation and errors
- **4.7** app store rating, up from 3.6
- A design system now used by **9 product teams**
